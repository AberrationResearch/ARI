ID #: ABR-004

Title: "The Oracle Machine"

Threat Classification: III Hazardous (HZ)

Description: ABR-004 appears to be a standard desktop computer of an undisclosed brand, manufactured in the year 2010. Upon physical inspection, no deviations from a non-anomalous model can be observed. The system runs an operating system that bears similarities to both Windows 10 and various Linux distributions. However, it is not based on any recognized software architecture.

The aberrationâ€™s peculiar properties manifest when the system is turned on and interacted with. ABR-004 has the ability to predict future technological advancements and spontaneously updates itself with hardware and software that is inconsistent with our current understanding of technology. It also stores files and documents purportedly from the future, most of which are encrypted or otherwise restricted.

Remarkably, ABR-004 does not require any external power source and functions indefinitely without any noticeable wear or need for maintenance.

Containment Protocols and Precautions: ABR-004 is to be stored in a secure containment chamber within ARI Site-7. Access is restricted to Level 3 researchers and above. The chamber must be lined with Faraday cage material to inhibit unauthorized wireless communications. All interaction and testing involving ABR-004 must be approved in writing by at least one Level 4 researcher.
