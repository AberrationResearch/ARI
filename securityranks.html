Aberration Research Institute (ARI) Security Clearance Rank List

  1. Clearance Level 6 (C6 - Censored and Unknown)
    The highest security clearance level within ARI, known only to a select few high-ranking officials. Information regarding C6 clearance is strictly compartmentalized to prevent unauthorized access.
  2. Clearance Level 5 (C5 - Executive Council)
    Members of the Executive Council possess C5 clearance, overseeing critical decision-making processes, classified information, and top-level initiatives related to aberration research, containment, and strategic planning.
  3. Clearance Level 4 (C4 - Division Heads)
    Division Heads hold C4 clearance, controlling departmental information, vital research data, and containment protocols relevant to their divisions. They are key decision-makers in their areas of expertise.
  4. Clearance Level 3 (C3 - Lead Researchers and Containment Specialists)
    C3 clearance is given to lead researchers, containment specialists, and agents in field operations and research. Access includes detailed operational information and sensitive data.
  5. Clearance Level 2 (C2 - Support Personnel)
    C2 clearance is assigned to support staff, including administrative, data analysts, security, and technical support. Access is restricted to information necessary for their duties.
  6. Clearance Level 1 (C1 - General Personnel)
    C1 clearance covers general personnel with access to basic information, involved in routine operations but not exposed to sensitive or highly classified data.
  7. Clearance Level 0 (C0 - Non-essential Personnel)
    C0 clearance is granted to non-essential personnel with limited access to critical information. Includes expendable personnel such as death row convicts, terminally ill individuals, and informed consent volunteers. Their involvement is regulated and overseen to minimize risks.
Note: Ethical Protocols regarding the use of expendable personnel are subject to ongoing review by a specialized Ethical Review Board within the ARI. This board ensures the well-being and consent of such individuals, with strict containment measures to prevent unnecessary harm. These practices are subject to internal scrutiny, and changes are made based on continuous evaluation of ethical considerations.

Personnel Advancement and Training

Clearance levels may be adjusted based on performance evaluation, specialized training, and individual qualifications. The ARI maintains a clear pathway for career progression, detailing the requirements and assessments necessary to advance through the security clearance ranks. Regular reviews and targeted training programs ensure that personnel are equipped to handle the responsibilities associated with higher clearance levels.
